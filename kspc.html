KSPC.org

My first experience with web development using Ruby on Rails.

"Gotta figure out what the customer wants"

At Harvey Mudd College every Computer Science major takes a course called Software Development, otherwise known as CS121. The objective of the course is to get students acquainted with real world software development. For a majority of the course we were split into groups of four and each group was assigned to a local organization or person within our community. My group, along with one other group, worked for KSPC, a college radio station in Claremont, CA. Our task was to create a new website for KSPC.

As project manager for the first half of the project I became the liasion between KSPC and my group. I communicated any questions my group and I had by email. I was also communicating our progress to the other group working for KSPC, whose product, a historic timeline, would be displayed on our website. Every week we would meet with a representative from KSPC. We would discuss what they wanted, our current progress and our goal for the upcoming week. During the project we worked in an agile environment.

We didn't realize what our client wanted until we were deep into the project; an aesthetically pleasing website that contained all of the customizations available through WordPress. The irony here is that we could've used WordPress, we didn't have to create a website from scratch. In hindsight, I realized it was our task to provide as many viable solutions as we could brainstorm to the client. Doing so would've saved us a lot of time.

Sadly, our website didn't end up as beautiful as we envisioned it. However, we were able to mimic a sample of the functionality that WordPress provides. We migrated KSPC's data to a relational database to display the old website's posts. Using our website KSPC was able to create new posts and edit old ones in a more intuitive manner than their previous process.

If you want to take a look at the website check out the KSPC repository on my GitHub (click the GitHub icon in the footer).
